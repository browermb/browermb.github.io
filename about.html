Projects

I am currently not working on any research projects. This past summer I worked on creating an autonomous golfcart to serve as a taxi system in a retirement community. This fall I am in a class revolving around the project. Please check back for updates on the cart.

The first project I worked on, I began developing an application for creating geometric constructions on the sphere with Dr. John Bowers. The goal of this project is to help mathematicians study circle-packing with visualizing and exploring inversive geometry. The application currently has constructions to draw a member of a coaxial family among others. You are welcome to follow my progress here

The other project I have worked on is called Wearables for Physical Therapy and is Dr. Jason Forsyth's project. Generally with physical therapy, a patient must go to an office to be observed and then must continue them at home. This provides a couple of issues: people might not be able to go see a physical therapist every week, they also might not do the exercises or they might not do them well. Wearables is a system that a patient will wear when performing their provided activies, it would then store this data, send it to the physical therapist, and provide feedback to the patient as to how they are doing and what they may need to correct.
